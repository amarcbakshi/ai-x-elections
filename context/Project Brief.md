# AI x Elections

A practitioner publication for the people who run American elections

---

**American election officials are navigating the most consequential midterm cycle in a generation almost entirely without a dedicated professional information source.** County and state administrators face a flood of AI-enabled voter challenges, tabulation crises with hard legislative deadlines, polling place consolidations with no impact analysis tools, and vendor pitches for AI software they have no framework to evaluate — all while relying on Signal threads and informal county-to-county calls to share knowledge that should be systematically organized and distributed. There is no trade publication for election administration. The gap is not subtle.

**The AI angle is not a marketing hook — it is the organizing lens.** Every major threat to election integrity in 2026 has an AI dimension: algorithmic voter roll challenges (Eagle AI, Ellie, the Cleta Mitchell ecosystem), AI-generated disinformation targeting officials directly, AI-powered GOTV operations creating new voter confusion scenarios at polling places, and the accelerating pace of administrative attacks that AI makes cheaper to coordinate and harder to attribute. "AI x elections" is also a deliberately non-partisan frame — it gives the publication license to cover anything election-relevant without triggering the partisanship filters that cause election officials to tune out advocacy-coded sources.

**Distribution is the strongest part of this project.** Gary Smith, who runs civitech.io and the BallotReady network, has direct email access to election officials across the country and has already signaled interest in co-branding or white-labeling the launch. This is not a distribution problem waiting to be solved — it is a solved distribution problem waiting to be used. The newsletter launches under a partnership or white-label arrangement with BallotReady, inheriting credibility and reach on day one.

**The newsletter doubles as a demand aggregator.** When election officials respond to coverage of a shared problem — the OCR scanner gap in Georgia, the absence of a polling place consolidation impact tool, the fragmented voter challenge response process — the publication has already built the audience into which a solution can be deployed. Media is the top of the funnel; tools are the follow-through. This is how Election Mayhem's civic infrastructure orientation translates into something operationally useful on a short timeline.

**The editorial model is the trade publication, not the advocacy newsletter.** Voice: practitioner-oriented, credible, specific. References: Politico Pro at its best, The Information, Puck. Anti-references: campaign email, Substack default, think-tank PDF. The publication is written for people who run elections, not people who are afraid of losing them. Fourteen bi-weekly issues launch June 1 and run through the morning of November 4.

**The compounding value is the channel itself.** A publication that earns the trust of election administrators nationwide becomes an owned distribution asset that outlasts the 2026 cycle. It is the foundation for a practitioner community, a tool deployment pipeline, and a media property with unique institutional reach into an audience that no other channel reliably accesses.

---

**Launch:** June 1, 2026
**Cadence:** Bi-weekly, 14 issues
**Audience:** County and state election officials (primary); journalists, civic tech practitioners, policy researchers (secondary)
**Distribution:** civitech.io / BallotReady partner network; independent newsletter growth
**Format:** Single-scroll email newsletter; editorial serif design; trade publication aesthetic
**Relationship to Election Mayhem:** AI x Elections is a practitioner-facing spin-out of the Election Mayhem beat. Election Mayhem covers election disruption for a general audience; AI x Elections serves the professionals who have to respond to it.
